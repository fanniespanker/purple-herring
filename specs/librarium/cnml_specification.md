# CNML Specification v0.5. (Draft)

## Foundations

CNML (Contextual Narrative Markup Language) is a constrained XML vocabulary with semantic rules.

All CNML documents MUST be well‑formed XML.

CNML is primarily an experiential and discourse-oriented representation system.

Experiential content refers to represented subjective or interpretive states, not objective truth claims.

CNML encodes semantic, experiential, structural, temporal, prosodic, and spatial relationships, not canonical presentation.

Evaluation, rendering, playback, and projection are interpreter-dependent realizations of CNML source structure.

Operational interpretation MAY derive additional structure for rendering or execution purposes, but such derived structure MUST NOT be treated as intrinsic source semantics.

---

## Core Evaluation Model

CNML evaluation operates across four primary expressive planes:

```text
Narrative × Temporal × Prosodic × Spatial
```

These planes are independently representable and compositional.

## Planes

| Plane     | Meaning                          |
|-----------|----------------------------------|
| Narrative | discourse, cognition, experience |
| Temporal  | musical/performance progression  |
| Prosodic  | affective modulation             |
| Spatial   | typographic/spatial arrangement  |

---

## Narrative Ontology

`<narrative>` does not represent objective reality.

Narrative content is interpreted as a projection generated by a narrator model.

Narrators MAY be:
- unreliable
- dishonest
- misleading
- hallucinating
- delusional
- temporally inconsistent
- partially reconstructive

CNML discourse is therefore epistemically unstable by default.

The root element MAY contain:

## Publication Layer

Publication-layer frames are optional organizational containers. Beyond the text itself, they may contain various metadata elements.

### Publication Frames

`<series>`
`<story>`
`<book>`

These define publication topology only.

### Publication Metadata Elements

Publication-layer frames SHOULD contain at least one of each of the following:
- `<title>`
- `<author>`
- `<date of="publication">`
- `<identifier scheme="identifier_scheme">`
- `<synopsis>`
- `<license>` — license information, which MUST contain one `<name>` element, and MAY contain any number of each of the following:
  - `<identifier scheme="identifier_scheme">` — an identifier (such as a URI/IRI) for the license; and
  - `<code>` — the legal code of the license; and
  - structured data that represents the terms of the license.
  
Publication frames may also contain any number of genre and audience elements:
- `<genre>`
- `<audience>`

Publication frames may also contain any number of subject elements which indicate the depth of those subjects' presence and/or the author's position (whether moral, academic, or otherwise) regarding those subjects:
- `<references>`
- `<depicts>`
- `<explores>`
- `<examines>`
- `<challenges>`
- `<opposes>`
- `<parodies>`

Publication frames may also embed or reference bibliographic data from other works through the `<external>` element, which contains `<work>` elements.

Examples include:

```xml
<book vocabulary="https://github.com/fanniespanker/Media-Description-Ontology/0.1/ns">

  <title>Hardboiled Whore</title>
  <author>Fannie Spanker</author>
  <date of="publication">2025</date>
  <identifier scheme="IRI">https://fanniespanker.com/armedescorts/hardboiledwhore/</identifier>
  <license>
    <name>Creative Commons Attribution Noncommercial ShareAlike 4.0 International</name>
    <iri>https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc-sa/4.0/legalcode</iri>
  </license>

  <synopsis style="diegetic">
    <heading>Some cops love themselves. Some cops know they’re human garbage.</heading>
  </synopsis>
  
  <genre resource="Fiction.humor/sex_comedy">sex comedy</genre>

  <parodies resource="#Columbo">Columbo</parodies>

  <supports resource="Politics.movements/labor/unions/activism">labor unions in bodily autonomy-oriented feminist activism</supports>

  <audience resource="People.marginalized_groups/GSM">gender and sexual minorities</audience>

  <work 
      about="#Columbo"
      medium="Media.medium/television"
      format="Media.format/series?of=Media.format/movie"
      resource="https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0061496"
  >
    <title>Columbo</title>
    <creator>Richard Levinson</creator>
    <creator>William Link</creator>
    <genre resource="Fiction.crime/mystery/procedural">police procedural mystery</genre>
    
    <character resource="https://id.loc.gov/authorities/sh93004873">
      <name>Columbo</name>
    </character>
  </work>
  
  ...
  
</book>
```

## Matter Layer

Matter elements partition reading experience.

```xml
<frontmatter>
<mainmatter>
<backmatter>
```

Matter elements:
- are structural
- are optional
- do not carry frame headers

## Narrative Structure Layer

Narrative structure elements organize experiential progression.

```xml
<act>
<chapter>
<scene>
```

### Containment

- `<act>` MAY appear only inside `<mainmatter>`
- `<chapter>` MAY appear only inside `<act>` or `<mainmatter>`
- `<scene>` MAY appear:
  - inside `<chapter>` or `<act>`
  - directly inside `<story>`

All are optional.

### Frame Headers

Zero or one of each of the following:

- `<title>`
- `<synopsis>`
- `<subheading>`

MAY appear once inside:
- `<series>`
- `<story>`
- `<book>`
- `<section>`
- `<act>`
- `<chapter>`
- `<scene>`

---

### Sections

```xml
<section>
```

Sections are general-purpose framing and segmentation units.

#### Allowed Locations

Sections MAY appear inside:
- `<frontmatter>`
- `<backmatter>`

Sections MAY contain zero or one of the following:
- `<title>`
- `<synopsis>`
- `<subheading>`

Sections MAY contain zero or more of the following:
- prose
- narrative content
- metadata

#### Role Attribute

```xml
<section role="">
```

The `role` attribute is optional and evaluator-defined.

Examples:
- dedication
- acknowledgments
- epigraph
- commentary
- notes
- appendix
- index

### Narrative Container

All primary experiential discourse SHOULD appear inside:

```xml
<narrative>
```

A narrative MAY contain:
- expression modalities
- prosody
- poems
- music
- nested narrative modalities

### Narrative Modalities

#### Narrative Modality Elements

<dream>
<fantasy>
<flashback>
<remember>

are sibling nodes under `<narrative>`, but NOT a single semantic taxonomy.

Instead, they form a 2×2 cognitive operator grid:

---

#### Cognitive Axes

##### Epistemic Source

| Value    | Meaning                        |
|----------|--------------------------------|
| irrealis | generated experiential content |
| memory   | retrieved experiential content |

##### Volition

| Value       | Meaning                  |
|-------------|--------------------------|
| involuntary | non-deliberate emergence |
| voluntary   | deliberate invocation    |

---

##### Mapping

| Element   | Source   | Volition    |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| dream     | irrealis | involuntary |
| fantasy   | irrealis | voluntary   |
| flashback | memory   | involuntary |
| remember  | memory   | voluntary   |

---

#### Formal Model

NarrativeMode = (EpistemicSource × Volition)

## Prosody

Prosody is a compositional modulation system.

### Dimensions

```xml
<quiet>     <loud>
<calm>      <excited>
<gentle>    <forceful>
<smooth>    <harsh>
<engaged>   <detached>
<emph>      <deemph>
```

Additionally, `<nonverbal>` suppresses implied verbal articulation, forming a virtual 7th dimension.

#### Semantics

Prosody:
- modifies expression quality
- does not alter discourse structure
- composes recursively
- is evaluator-interpreted

---

### Integrity Operators

Integrity operators instruct the evaluator to decorate or alter their text to appear degraded according to aesthetic rules determined by the renderer's configuration. These indicate *diegetic* corruption of representation, whether intentional or unintentional.

These are:

```xml
<redacted>
<censored>
<corrupted>
```

#### Semantics

The `mode` attribute is evaluator-defined and MAY correspond to recognizable corruption aesthetics or encoding artifacts. The `severity` attribute is evaluator defined and MAY correspond to corruption that is more or less intense.

Examples:

`<redacted severity="0.8" source="narrator">Black bars and footnotes abound.</redacted>`
…renders as something like `█████ₐ₄₋₃₁ ████ₙ₁₁₋₂ and █████████ₙ₇₋₁₃ ███████ᵥ₅₋₁.

`<censored severity="0.4" source="government">You wouldn't download an Internet.</censored>`
…renders as something like `You wouldn't [CENSORED] an [CENSORED].`

`<corrupted severity="1.0" mode="mojibake">UTF-8 tastes great.</corrupted>`
…renders as something like: `ॕൔᡆᔭጸ ٴѡࡳѴݥᡳᄠg౲ὥᝡ୴ᐮ`

`<corrupted severity="0.3" mode="zalgo">All hail Zalgo!</corrupted>`
…renders as something like `Ä̙̹͆ll̪̺̝ hail̗̲̍ͧ ͅZaḷ̮͓̈́̆͆g͔͚̳̈͑ͫȍ̯̘̍!͐͗`

## Rendering Model

CNML MAY be rendered into:
- HTML
- PDF
- TeX/LaTeX DVI
- SVG
- EPUB
- Markdown
- PostScript
- OpenDocument
- audio (text-to-speech, music)
- interactive runtimes

### Recommended HTML Projection

| CNML            | HTML       |
|-----------------|------------|
| narrative units | `<p>`      |
| prosody         | `<span>`   |
| poem/music      | inline SVG |

# Constraints

- CNML documents MUST be well-formed XML
- Inline elements compose recursively
- No cross-boundary inheritance is implied unless evaluator-defined
